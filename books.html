Despite her youth, Sarah White has quickly become one of the government’s most promising young employees following the great war, rising through the ranks of the Forestry Department, where she tries to salvage what is left of her country’s beautiful woodlands. Everyone is overjoyed with her work, except the President- and in a world where her word is the only one that matters, Sarah might as well be just another peasant. Though she tries to keep her head down and do her work, she stumbles upon a terrible secret, one that sends her on the run through her beloved forced, a squad of government-employed poisoners following her every step, releasing chemicals into the air that can destroy villages and caue entire acres to shrivel up and die. Will Sarah use her information for good? Or will she let her fellow citizens, not to mention the woods she loves, perish?

Ken Liu’s The Festival of Dreams is a high fantasy novel brimming with decadence and deception, an engrossing tale unfolding over generations to a thrilling ending. A former peasant, NAME is thrilled in her new role as queen, excited to see the inner workings of the Kingdom she is now part of. During the annual festival between Kingdoms, done to appease the old Gods, she makes a shocking discovery that sets her on a path towards vengeance- against her enemies, her family, and the gods themselves.

England, 1862. Darwin’s theories have rocked the world, no more so than at Oxford University, where a group of ambitious young naturalists are forced to reevaluate everything they thought was true. In the pursuit of knowledge, the group sets sail down the Amazon River to create a document heretofore thought impossible: a comprehensive list of all creatures in the area. It is there that they make a shocking discovery, a cover of mermaids who defy the natural world and the ideals of science all in one. If the two groups are two survive, they have to learn to trust each other.
